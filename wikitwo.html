1. Gaganyaan: India’s Human Spaceflight Program Overview

Gaganyaan is ISRO’s ambitious human spaceflight program aimed at sending Indian astronauts, or Vyomanauts, to space. It will make India the fourth country to independently conduct human space missions, following the US, Russia, and China.

⦁ Planned Launch: 2025

⦁ Landing Date: August 23, 2023

⦁ Mission Duration: 5-7 days in low Earth orbit (LEO)

⦁ Orbit Altitude: Approximately 400 km

⦁ Budget: ₹10,000 crore (~$1.3 billion)

2. Mission Objectives

Human Spaceflight: Successfully send and return a crewed mission to space.

Technological Development: Demonstrate indigenous capabilities for human space exploration.

Scientific Research: Conduct experiments in microgravity to benefit various fields, including material science and medicine.

2.Key Components

Crew Module (Orbital Module):

A pressurized capsule designed to carry three astronauts.

⦁ Equipped with environmental control and life support systems (ECLSS).

⦁ Features a heat shield to withstand re-entry temperatures of over 2,000°C.

Service Module:

⦁ Houses propulsion systems, power systems, and avionics.

⦁Provides support for the crew module during the mission.Launch Vehicle (GSLV Mk III):

⦁ Also known as LVM-3, ISRO’s most powerful rocket, customized for crewed missions.

⦁ Ensures a safe and smooth ascent to orbit.

Key Milestones Uncrewed Test Missions:

⦁ Gaganyaan-1: Planned for 2024 to test the launch vehicle and crew module systems.

⦁ Includes a robotic payload equipped with sensors to mimic human responses.

Abort Missions:

⦁ Demonstrated crew escape systems during emergencies.

Successful pad abort and in-flight abort tests conducted in 2023 and 2024.

Astronaut Training:

⦁ Conducted in collaboration with Russia’s Gagarin Cosmonaut Training Center and France’s CNES.

⦁ Includes simulations, zero-gravity training, and survival exercises.

3. Technological Innovations

⦁ Development of an indigenous life support system for astronaut safety.

⦁ Advanced avionics and software for real-time mission control.

⦁ Implementation of Crew Escape System (CES) to ensure astronaut safety during emergencies.

Global Collaboration

⦁ Partnerships with Russia, France, and private space firms for training and technology.

⦁ Opens avenues for joint human space exploration in the future.

Future Prospects

⦁ A stepping stone for ISRO’s long-term vision of building a space station by 2035.
